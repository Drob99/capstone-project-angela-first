About Me

This experience strengthened my belief that AI can transform the way we live, work, and learn—and I want to be part of that transformation.

One of my proudest accomplishments has been participating in undergraduate research initiatives, like taking a course in undergraduate research. Although I am still new to research, I have found excitement in asking hard questions, reviewing literature, and contributing to new ideas. I believe research is more than a requirement — it is a mindset that pushes us to think critically, communicate clearly, and stay curious. I am especially interested in using AI for impactful applications, such as education, accessibility, and health.

Beyond the classroom, I enjoy building projects that apply what I learn. Whether I am working with Python, experimenting with PyTorch, debugging a C++ application, or managing code on GitHub, I love the process of turning an idea into a working solution. I am also exploring UI/UX design and web development to become a more versatile engineer.

I am someone who thrives on structure and growth. While I have faced moments of doubt or felt like I was not doing “enough” (and I still am), I have learned that progress is about consistency. I do not have all the answers yet, but I show up every day to learn, improve, and build things I am proud of.

Outside of academics, I enjoy chess (with a rapid rating around 2100), watching anime, reading, and having meaningful conversations. I am also passionate about helping others — I have mentored peers, collaborated on group projects, and supported friends through their own learning journeys. I believe that personal growth happens not only through competition but also through community.

Looking ahead, I am preparing for graduate studies or a future in tech roles that challenge me intellectually and align with my values. Whether it is pursuing a Master’s or Ph.D. at KAUST or contributing to forward-thinking companies like Google or Microsoft, I am eager to keep learning and growing. I believe my foundation in software engineering, combined with a genuine drive to explore the unknown, will allow me to make meaningful contributions — both as a student and a future researcher or engineer.
